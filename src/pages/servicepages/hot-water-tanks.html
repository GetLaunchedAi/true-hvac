FAQ’s

Hot Water Tank FAQs

Get answers to common questions about water heaters and maintenance.

  • Most traditional tank-style water heaters last between 8 and 12 years. If your tank is over 10 years old, it's a good idea to start planning for a replacement before it fails.

  • A leak from the bottom often indicates that the internal tank has corroded through, which usually means the unit needs to be replaced. However, it could also be a faulty drain valve or pressure relief valve.

  • Tankless water heaters provide endless hot water on demand, take up much less space, and are significantly more energy-efficient than traditional tank models.
